Spondyloarthropathy
Note: This decision tool assumes a chronic (6+ weeks), inflammatory, articular disorder that affects more than 3 joints asymmetrically.

Findings in Spondyloarthropathies
Location/Distribution
1. Asymmetric
2. Predominantly lower extremity
3. Back pain of insidious onset of more than 3 months duration associated with morning stiffness and improvement with activity
<-- QUESTION 1: Is the location/distribution of pain/symptoms in any of the patterns listed above (1-3)?
Comorbid conditions
1. Psoriasis
2. Inflammatory bowel disease
<-- QUESTION 2: Are either of the comorbid conditions above (1-2) present?
Other factors
1. sacroiliitis
2. enthesopathy
3. dactylitis
4. buttock pain (unilateral or alternating)
5. urethritis or cervicitis
6. family history of spondyloarthropathy
7. iritis
8. Acute diarrhea or nongonococcal urethritis within 1 mo of onset
<-- QUESTION 3: Are any of the other factors listed above (1-8) present?
Spondylitis: Inflammatory spinal pain and limitation of movement
