CHADS2 Score, Stroke Risk in Atrial Fibrillation[html]
<strong>Summary:</strong>
The CHADS2 score is used to help decide whether or not to anticoagulant. An approach to the decision of warfarin for oral anticoagulation for atrial fibrillation is suggested in <a href="http://www.aafp.org/afp/2005/0615/afp20050615p2348-f1.pdf" target="_blank">Am Fam Physician. 2005 Jun 15;71(12):2348-2350.</a>1) Use the <a href="http://www.soapnote.org/cardiovascular/chads2/" target="_blank">CHADS2 rule</a> as well as the <a href="http://www.soapnote.org/cardiovascular/accp-stroke-risk-calculator/" target="_blank">ACCP rule</a> to determine the risk of stroke.
2) If both these measures predict moderate risk of stroke, proceed to calculate the <a href="http://www.soapnote.org/cardiovascular/outpatient-bleeding-risk-index-obri/" target="_blank">Outpatient Bleeding Risk Index</a> and weigh the risks versus the benefits.[/html]
